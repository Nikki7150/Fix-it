## Inspiration
I was inspired by the feeling of overthinking—how one small thought can spiral into thousands more—and how trying to "fix" it often tends to make it worse. I also added a kind of perfectionism spin to it by not letting the user completely fix all the issues, thus leaving it "imperfect". I often go through these feelings so I came up witht eh idea of making it visual to show people who dont go through it how it feels. I wanted to turn those invisible and intense mental experiences into something interactive and visual. 

## What it does
"Fix it" is an interactive web experience where users try to fix the thoughts in their head. Each time they try to fix one thought, that thought gets fixed but many more appear, creating a growing sense of chaos and crowding which is not real. The progress bar also gives the illution of improvement and control, but it doesn't actually reach 100% perfection thus reinforcin the idea that perfection is unreachable. 

## How I built it
I built this website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The thought bubbles are dynamically generated using JavaScript, with random positioning, text messages, seizes, and shapes. The user interactions, like clicking on the "Fix" button, pops the bubble, triggers new bubbles, and updates the progress bar. I used a lot of CSS for the animations, transitions, and visual effects like glassmorphism (lowkey had to search up a word for this) and motions. 

## Challenges I ran into
One challenge I faced was balancing the chaos with the usability and making the experience feel overwhelming without being completely unplayable. Positioning the elements dynamically without breaking the entire layout was also very tricky, using rectWidth and rectHeight. Another challenge I faced was making the progression of the experience feel more intentional rather than just random. I mostly had issues with layout, positioning, and how to make the actions seem natural and flow together but I persevered and made it work out in the end. 

## Accomplishments that I'm proud of
I'm very proud of how the experience evolves emotionally—from calm and controlled to chaotic and overwhelming. I really like the interactive design and how it reflects the concepts I wanted to get across successfull, especially the visual details like the animations and movements and the misleading progress bar. 

## What I learned
I learned how powerful every small UI/UX decision can be inshaping the entire trajectory of the user experience. I learned to create better animations using keyframes and media queries to make my designs feel more natural than blocky. I also improved my ability to structure dynamic elements in JavaScript and creating more intentional designs that just functional ones.
